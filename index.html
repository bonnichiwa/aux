Project Outline

“You’re almost always wrong about your customers”

- A bad UI/UX Designer

Two of the most integral and yet often overlooked aspects of any app are the User Interface and the subsequent User Experience. Too often user’s are left confused and frustrated by convulated designs, poorly thought out features and unnatural elements, all constrained by unimaginitive and repetitive design trends.

Here at Ordr, design, creativity and the user experience are all part of our core ethos. From our versatile logo to our complex and yet beautifually simple UI, we have always endeavoured to creating an intuitive and captivating experience and one that will set the standard for years to come. Keeping this in mind, while using our website, current app design, your own research into past, current and upcoming desgin trends and your personal design ethos, please put together a “look book” for how you believe our articles should look like.

Case Study Submission

My approach to this was to utilize the most user-friendly navigation system, simple yet highly appealing designs. I started by first researching about the company's goals and philosophy. From there I determined that there were 3 different elements to take into consideration:

  • Newsfeed product
  • Friend's posts on trending items
  • Curated posts section based on your account

These would be 3 main landing screens, and then it would go deeper one level into the 'post' that would be featured in each of these screens. I wanted to have the transitions seamless and require the least amount of effort, so simple screen slides to the right and left, screen pulled down, simple tap to read an overlay of the story or to view the gallery.

The categories would be simply colour coded so users have an easy reference, and bookmarking a story would be as simple as a tap. The stories would be ordered chronologically, and I had different screen layouts for these stories to illustrate how they could be displayed differently. This would be decided upon best user experience and what would be better for viewing - large banner like images or small cards which would provide the information just as efficiently.
